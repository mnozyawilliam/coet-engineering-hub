SC223

Structural Analysis II

Dr. Mwakalinga · Dept. of Structural & Construction Engineering · 4 Credit Hours · Semester 2

About this course

Builds on SC222 to cover indeterminate structures, deflection methods, and an introduction to matrix stiffness analysis, with weekly problem sets tied to real structural design cases.

Learning outcomes

  • Analyse statically indeterminate beams and frames
  • Apply force and displacement methods to solve structures
  • Use strain energy methods, including Castigliano's theorem
  • Interpret results against real design codes

Lab & Design Project

Degree of indeterminacy, compatibility conditions, and where the force method is headed this semester.
Redundant selection, primary structures, and compatibility equations for propped and fixed beams.
Extending the force method to rigid frames with multiple redundants.
Deriving slope-deflection equations and applying them to continuous beams.
Distribution factors, carry-over, and iterative balancing for frames and beams.
Learning objectives
  • Compute strain energy for axial, bending, and torsional members
  • Apply Castigliano's theorem to find deflections
  • Use the unit-load method as a cross-check
Reflection — Where else in structural design have you seen an energy-based method used instead of a force-balance method?
